# Website Quality Assessment: THTX.nl

**URL:** https://thtx.nl/
**Date:** 2025-11-26
**Overall Score:** 47/60 (78%) - **Grade B**

## Executive Summary

THTX.nl demonstrates strong messaging clarity and authentic positioning for a consultancy launch. The site confidently owns its pre-launch startup stage, uses specific target audience language ("visionairs die 10X denken"), and provides concrete examples of value. However, design inconsistencies, competitive CTAs, and some visual credibility concerns prevent it from reaching A-grade execution. The Dutch-language focus is appropriate for the target market.

---

## Category Scores

### I. Messaging & Clarity: 15/18 ⭐

**A. Specificity in Headline: 3/3** ✅
"Voor visionairs die 10X denken, niet 10%" is highly specific. Uses the target audience's conceptual framework (10X vs 10% thinking) rather than generic business speak. Immediately distinguishes from incremental improvement consultancies.

**B. User-Centric Language: 3/3** ✅
Speaks directly in language the target audience uses:
- "AI Solutions Architect" (technical jargon)
- "Interne AI-capaciteit" (their framing)
- "IT-business kloof overbruggen" (their pain point)
- "Van idee naar werkende oplossing in 90 dagen" (outcome-focused)

**C. Clear Audience Definition: 2/3** ⚠️
Clear this is for business leaders/professionals seeking transformation, but could be more explicit. "Visionairs" is evocative but slightly vague. Would benefit from "Voor CTOs en transformatieleiders die..." for +1 point.

**D. Literal Sub-Headline: 3/3** ✅
Excellent supporting copy: "Bouw interne AI-capaciteit die blijft en groeit. Transformeer hoe je organisatie waarde creëert met AI Solutions Architecture." Clearly explains what they do beyond the provocative headline.

**E. Focused Messaging: 2/3** ⚠️
Mostly focused but quite long (full-page scroll site). Some sections compete for attention (quiz, manifesto, multiple case examples). Acts more as a complete sales page than a "front page" guiding deeper exploration.

**F. Naming Simplicity: 2/3** ⚠️
Minor confusion: THTX (brand) vs "AI Solutions Architect" (movement) vs "Track 1/Track 2" (programs). Not critical, but three different conceptual frames require mental mapping.

### II. Design & Aesthetic Quality: 10/15 ⚠️

**A. Avoidance of "AI Slop": 2/3** ⚠️
Mostly original but shows some AI-era design patterns:
- ✅ No purple gradients or massive shadows

- ⚠️ Light blue backgrounds (#def0fa, #dcebf3) feel generic

Generally consistent but notable variations:

- Inconsistent use of icons vs. images

**D. Contrast and Legibility: 2/3** ⚠️
Generally good but some issues:
- Yellow text (#d4db3e) on white backgrounds has adequate contrast
- Yellow buttons on light blue backgrounds work well
- ⚠️ Some smaller text could be darker for better readability
- ✅ Large headers are highly legible

**E. Professionalism / Trust Signal: 2/3** ⚠️
Mixed signals:
- ✅ Clean, modern design
- ✅ Confidently owns pre-launch/startup stage
- ⚠️ Some sections feel busy (many competing elements)
- ⚠️ Early-bird badges and crossed-out pricing feels slightly promotional rather than enterprise

### III. User Experience & Conversion Flow: 14/18 ⭐

**A. Clear Primary CTA: 2/3** ⚠️
Generally clear but some competition:
- Hero: Two CTAs ("Zie wat mogelijk is" + "Lees het manifesto")
- Navigation: "Reserveer plek" consistently visible ✅
- Sections have focused CTAs
- Quiz section adds another conversion path (self-qualification)
- Would be stronger with single dominant CTA per screen

**B. Minimal Distractions: 3/3** ✅
Hero section is clean and focused. No distracting animations, auto-playing videos, or moving elements. Message stays central.

**C. Conversational UI Integrity: 3/3** ✅
No chat widgets or elements that could be confused with cookie banners. Quiz section is clearly intentional and well-integrated.

**D. Accessible Demo/Trial: 1/3** ❌
Major weakness:
- No self-serve trial or demo available
- Only CTA is "Reserveer plek" or email contact
- For €1,750-€25,000 programs, no lower-friction entry point
- No lead magnet, free resources, or sample workshop content
- Pre-launch status explains this partially, but hurts conversion

**E. Showcasing Workflows: 2/3** ⚠️
Good attempt with 4-step timeline (Probleem → Prototype → Tests → Deploy) but:
- Text-based rather than visual workflow
- Case study shows problem → solution → impact well
- Missing: actual AI Solutions Architecture methodology visuals
- No flowcharts, diagrams, or process illustrations

**F. Timely Monetization: 3/3** ✅
Excellent value-first approach:
- Hero focuses on transformation promise
- Case study demonstrates concrete value
- Pricing only appears deep in the page (#tracks section)
- Early-bird pricing softens the ask
- Launch guarantee reduces risk

- "72% winstgevender en 58% sneller" - Appears to be cited research but no source

**D. Focus on Expertise: 0/3** ❌
Major gap:

- No "About" or team page
- No expertise signals (previous work, credentials, domain knowledge)
- No author attribution
- For €25K programs, lack of team visibility hurts credibility

## Priority Improvements

### 1. **Add Team/Founder Expertise Section** (Critical for credibility)
**Rationale:** For high-ticket B2B services (€1,750-€25,000), buyers need to know who they're working with. Even a brief founder section with relevant experience (AI implementation, business transformation, specific industry expertise) would add significant credibility.

**Implementation:** Add simple section with photo + 2-3 sentence bio emphasizing relevant domain expertise. Could go in footer or before contact section.

---

### 2. **Create Low-Friction Lead Magnet** (Critical for conversion)
**Rationale:** €1,750 early-bird is a significant ask without any lower-friction entry point. Visitors need a way to engage before committing to paid programs.

**Options:**
- Free "10X AI Readiness Assessment" (15-minute online tool)
- Downloadable "AI Solutions Architecture Canvas" (PDF framework)
- Free 60-minute webinar: "Van 10% naar 10X: AI Transformatie Cases"
- Email series: "5 Dagen 10X Denken Masterclass"
- Newsletter with case studies and frameworks

**Implementation:** Add CTA in hero and throughout site: "Start met gratis readiness check" → Captures email + qualifies leads

---

### 3. **Strengthen Visual Credibility with Process Visuals** (Critical for clarity)
**Rationale:** Text-heavy case studies and methodology explanations would be more compelling with visual workflows, diagrams, or mockups. Helps technical buyers understand the approach.

**Implementation:**
- Create visual process diagram for 4-step timeline
- Add workflow diagram for AI Solutions Architecture methodology
- Consider mockup screenshots of example outputs (even conceptual)
- Add simple infographics for key statistics

## Detailed Category Analysis

### Messaging Strengths
The site's core messaging is its strongest asset. The 10X vs 10% framing immediately differentiates from typical consultancies focused on incremental improvement. The language consistently speaks to transformation-minded leaders, and the supporting copy effectively balances aspiration with concrete methodology.

### Design Opportunities
While professional, the design could be more distinctive. Consider:
- More consistent card/component styling
- Richer visual language (custom illustrations vs. generic icons)
- Stronger visual hierarchy in dense sections
- More white space in busy areas (possibilities section, tracks comparison)

### Conversion Path Gaps
The single biggest opportunity is creating stepping stones before the €1,750 commitment. Even with early-bird pricing, that's a significant ask for first-time visitors. A lead magnet would dramatically improve conversion while also qualifying leads.

### Credibility Considerations
For B2B services at this price point, buyers typically want to know:
- Who's leading the workshops?
- What's their track record?
- What relevant expertise do they bring?

Even a minimal team section would significantly improve trust, especially for risk-averse enterprise buyers.

---

## Recommendations by Priority

**Must Fix (Before Launch):**
1. Add founder/team expertise section
2. Create at least one lead magnet/free resource
3. Add sources or context for bold claims (ROI statistics)

**Should Improve (Pre-Launch Nice-to-Have):**
4. Create visual process diagrams
5. Simplify competing CTAs (focus on one primary action)
6. Strengthen visual consistency (component system)

**Could Enhance (Post-Launch):**
7. Add client testimonials as they come in
8. Create more detailed case studies
9. Add video testimonials or workshop footage
10. Expand resources section (blog, tools, frameworks)

## Final Assessment

**Grade: B (47/60 - 78%)**

THTX.nl is a solid B-grade execution that's 80% of the way to excellence. The messaging is exceptional, the positioning is clear, and the value proposition is well-articulated. The main gaps are in credibility signals (team expertise) and conversion optimization (lead magnets).

For a pre-launch consultancy, this is strong work. With the three priority improvements above, this could easily reach A-grade (51+) before the February 2026 launch.

The site successfully differentiates, clearly communicates value, and sets appropriate expectations. It just needs to build more trust through team visibility and lower the barrier to engagement through lead magnets.

**Key Insight:** The product (workshops) is well-explained. Now show *who* is delivering them and give visitors a way to experience your thinking before committing €1,750+.
